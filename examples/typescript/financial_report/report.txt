1. AAPL (Apple Inc.)
   - Profile: Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. As of March 2023, Apple is the world's biggest company by market capitalization, and with US$394.3 billion the largest technology company by 2022 revenue.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: The COVID-19 pandemic has had, and continues to have, a significant impact around the world, prompting governments and businesses to take unprecedented measures, such as restrictions on travel and business operations, temporary closures of businesses, and quarantine and shelter-in-place orders. The COVID-19 pandemic has at times significantly curtailed global economic activity and caused significant volatility and disruption in global financial markets. The COVID-19 pandemic and the measures taken by many countries in response have adversely affected and could in the future materially adversely impact the Company's business, results of operations, financial condition. Certain partners, suppliers, and logistical service providers have experienced disruptions during the COVID-19 pandemic, resulting in supply shortages.
     
2. AMZN (Amazon.com, Inc.)
   - Profile: Amazon.com, Inc. is an American multinational technology company focusing on e-commerce, cloud computing, online advertising, digital streaming, and artificial intelligence. It has been often referred to as "one of the most influential economic and cultural forces in the world", and is often regarded as one of the world's most valuable brands.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: Macroeconomic factors, including inflation, increased interest rates, significant capital market volatility, the prolonged COVID-19 pandemic, global supply chain constraints, and global economic and geopolitical developments, have direct and indirect impacts on Amazon's results of operations. The COVID-19 pandemic and any future pandemic or health crisis could impact Amazon's future operations, including changes in government laws and regulations, disruptions in supply chain and logistics, and volatility in global financial markets.
    
3. NVDA (Nvidia Corporation)
   - Profile: Nvidia Corporation is an American multinational technology company incorporated in Delaware and based in Santa Clara, California. It is a software and fabless company which designs graphics processing units (GPUs), application programming interfaces (APIs) for data science and high-performance computing, as well as system on a chip units (SoCs) for the mobile computing and automotive market. Nvidia is a dominant supplier of artificial intelligence hardware and software.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: The pandemic resurfaces, such as lockdown measures due to COVID-19 containment efforts, have negatively impacted Nvidia's end customer sales in China. COVID-19-related disruptions have created and may continue to create supply chain and logistics constraints. The COVID-19 pandemic has affected and could continue to have a material adverse impact on Nvidia's financial condition and results of operations. Nvidia modified its business and workforce practices in response to COVID-19, including flexible work and social distancing measures.
     
4. UNH (UnitedHealth Group Incorporated)
   - Profile: UnitedHealth Group Incorporated is an American multinational managed healthcare and insurance company based in Minnetonka, Minnesota. Offering insurance services under UnitedHealthcare and healthcare products under Optum, it is the world's eleventh-largest company by revenue and the largest healthcare company by revenue, and the largest insurance company by net premiums.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: Large-scale medical emergencies, pandemics (such as COVID-19), and other extreme events could result in public health crises or otherwise have a material adverse effect on UnitedHealth Group's business operations. COVID-19-related disruptions have created and may continue to create supply chain and logistics constraints. Public health crises arising from natural disasters and effects of climate change could impact UnitedHealth Group's business operations and increase medical care costs.
     
5. JNJ (Johnson & Johnson)
   - Profile: Johnson & Johnson (J&J) is an American multinational pharmaceutical and medical technologies corporation headquartered in New Brunswick, New Jersey.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: Johnson & Johnson acknowledges that the COVID-19 pandemic has adversely impacted its business, results of operations, and financial condition, including lower sales and reduced customer demand for certain products. The extent to which COVID-19, or other health crises, could impact J&J's future operations depends on various factors, and the company has robust business continuity plans in place to mitigate potential impacts. J&J has implemented measures to ensure the health and safety of employees in the workplace and continues to support employees during the pandemic.
     
6. MRK (Merck & Co., Inc.)
   - Profile: Merck & Co., Inc. is an American multinational pharmaceutical company headquartered in Rahway, New Jersey, and is named for Merck Group, founded in Germany in 1668, of whom it was once the American arm.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: Merck acknowledges that the global COVID-19 pandemic has negatively impacted its business, operations, financial performance, and prospects. COVID-19-related disruptions have affected Merck's results of operations since the start of the pandemic. The company is focused on the health and safety of its employees and has implemented measures to address the impact of the pandemic on its operational and financial performance.
   
7. PEP (PepsiCo, Inc.)
   - Profile: PepsiCo, Inc. is an American multinational food, snack, and beverage corporation headquartered in Harrison, New York. PepsiCo's business encompasses all aspects of the food and beverage market. It oversees the manufacturing, distribution, and marketing of its products.
   - Recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic: PepsiCo has faced challenges in 2022, including supply chain disruptions, inflationary cost environment, adverse weather conditions, and the COVID-19 pandemic. The company continues to monitor changes in the retail landscape and seeks to build its global e-commerce and digital capabilities. Despite challenges, PepsiCo has implemented various safety protocols to ensure the health and safety of its associates and has focused on fostering employee engagement during this critical time.
